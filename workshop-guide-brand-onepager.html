STEP 00

Before We Start

5 minutes

Let's make sure everyone has what they need. You only need three things for this workshop: a Claude AI account, a browser, and a photo of yourself (optional but recommended).

SETUP CHECKLIST

  1. Open claude.ai in your browser and sign in (free account works)
  2. Have a professional photo of yourself ready (saved on your computer)
  3. Open a second browser tab — we'll preview our page here later
  4. Keep this guide open — you'll copy prompts from it

TIP: LANGUAGE

All prompts in this guide are in English, but Claude understands Slovak perfectly. You can answer Claude's questions in Slovak, and you can ask it to write your content in Slovak at any point. Just add "Write in Slovak" to any prompt.

STEP 01

Brand Discovery

10 minutes

Before we design anything, we need to figure out who you are professionally. The best personal brands aren't made up — they're discovered. We'll let AI help us pull out the interesting parts of your story.

PROMPT 1 — THE INTERVIEW
You are a personal brand strategist. Your job is to interview me so you can later help me create a personal brand landing page. Ask me 8-10 questions, one at a time. Wait for my answer before asking the next question. Focus on: - What I do professionally and what makes me different - My key achievements and projects I'm proud of - Who I help and what problems I solve - My personality and communication style - My goals — what do I want people to do after visiting my page Start with the first question now.

Claude will now interview you. Answer each question honestly — don't overthink it. The more authentic your answers, the better your brand page will be. This conversation typically takes 5-8 minutes.

YOUR TURN

Copy the prompt above into Claude and go through the full interview. Answer all questions. When Claude finishes, move to the next step.

IF YOU GET STUCK

Don't worry about giving "perfect" answers. Short, real answers are better than polished corporate speak. Think about what you'd tell a friend at a coffee shop, not a job interviewer.

STEP 02

Visual Identity

8 minutes

Now we'll ask Claude to suggest a visual style for your brand — colors, fonts, and overall mood. This step is what makes your page feel uniquely yours, not like a generic template.

PROMPT 2 — VISUAL STYLE
Based on everything you've learned about me from the interview, suggest a visual identity for my personal brand page. Give me: 1. A color palette (primary color, secondary color, background, text color) — include hex codes 2. A font pairing (one for headings, one for body text) from Google Fonts 3. The overall mood/vibe of the design (e.g., "bold and modern", "warm and minimal") 4. A brief explanation of WHY these choices fit my brand Present this as a clear, organized brand brief I can use.

Review what Claude suggests. If something doesn't feel right, tell it. For example: "I like the colors but make it darker" or "I'd prefer something more minimalist." This is how you refine AI output — you don't have to accept the first suggestion.

PROMPT 2B — REFINE (OPTIONAL)
I like the direction but [your feedback here]. Can you adjust the color palette and explain the updated choices?

YOUR TURN

Get your visual identity from Claude. Iterate at least once — change something, ask for an alternative. This teaches you an important AI skill: prompt refinement.

STEP 03

Content Generation

10 minutes

Time to create the actual text for your page. We'll ask Claude to write your headline, bio, project descriptions, and call-to-action — all based on the interview and brand identity we've already established.

PROMPT 3 — PAGE CONTENT
Now write the content for my personal brand one-pager. Based on the interview and visual identity, create: 1. **Hero section:** - A short professional label (3-4 words, e.g., "Marketing · E-commerce · AI") - A compelling headline (2-3 words per line, max 3 lines) - A tagline (one sentence that captures what I do) - A short bio paragraph (3-4 sentences, first person) 2. **About section:** - 2 paragraphs about my professional journey and approach - A list of 5-7 key skills/expertise areas 3. **Projects section:** - For each project or service I mentioned, write: - Project name - A 2-3 sentence description - A call-to-action link text 4. **Contact section:** - A short invitation to connect (2 sentences) Keep the tone confident but approachable. Make it concise — this is a one-pager, not a resume.

This is where most people get excited — seeing AI write about them in a compelling way. But don't just accept it blindly. Read every sentence and ask yourself: "Is this true? Is this me?"

PROMPT 3B — ITERATE ON TONE
The content is good but I want to adjust the tone. Can you: - Make the bio more [casual / confident / humble / bold] - Shorten the about section — make each paragraph max 2 sentences - Make the project descriptions more action-oriented Keep everything else the same.

YOUR TURN

Generate your page content. Then iterate at least once on the tone or length. Remember: AI gives you a great first draft, but your edits make it authentic.

STEP 04

Build the HTML Page

15 minutes

This is the magic moment. We'll ask Claude to take everything — the brand identity, the content, the visual style — and generate a complete, working HTML page. No coding required from you.

PROMPT 4 — GENERATE HTML
Now create a complete, single-file HTML page for my personal brand using everything we've built: the brand identity, color palette, fonts, and all the content. Requirements: - Single HTML file with all CSS in a <style> tag (no external files) - Use the exact color palette and Google Fonts we chose - Dark background with a subtle grid pattern overlay - Responsive design that works on mobile and desktop - Sections: Hero with photo placeholder, About with skills, Projects as cards, Contact with social links - Include a LinkedIn icon link to: [your LinkedIn URL] - Smooth scroll navigation - Fade-in animations on scroll using IntersectionObserver - Photo: use a placeholder div with my initials — I'll add the real photo later - Footer with a small "Built with BrandxAI" badge Design style: professional, dark theme, clean spacing, rounded cards with subtle borders. Make it look like it was designed by a professional — this is going live on the internet.

Claude will generate a full HTML page. Here's what to do with it:

PREVIEW YOUR PAGE

  1. Copy the entire HTML code Claude generates
  2. Open any text editor (Notepad on Windows, TextEdit on Mac — switch to plain text mode)
  3. Paste the code and save the file as index.html
  4. Double-click the file to open it in your browser
  5. You should see your personal brand page!

Look at your page in the browser. If something doesn't look right, tell Claude. You can be specific or vague — both work.

PROMPT 4B — FIX & ADJUST
The page looks good but I need these changes: - [e.g., "Make the hero section taller"] - [e.g., "Change the font size of the bio — it's too small"] - [e.g., "Add more space between sections"] Give me the complete updated HTML file.

ADDING YOUR PHOTO

To add your photo: save your photo in the same folder as your index.html file, name it photo.jpg, then find the placeholder div in the HTML and replace it with: <img src="photo.jpg" alt="Your Name">

STEP 05

Go Live

10 minutes

Your page exists on your computer. Now let's put it on the internet so anyone can see it. We'll use GitHub to store your code and Vercel to host it — both are free and this is the same stack that my consulting site (consulting.michalfarkas.com) runs on. The big advantage: every time you edit a file on GitHub, Vercel automatically re-deploys your site in seconds — no manual uploads, no FTP, no hassle.

PART A — CREATE A GITHUB REPOSITORY

  1. Go to github.com and sign in (or create a free account)
  2. Click the + icon in the top right → New repository
  3. Name it something like my-brand or personal-site
  4. Keep it Public (required for free Vercel hosting)
  5. Check "Add a README file"
  6. Click Create repository

PART B — UPLOAD YOUR FILES

  1. In your new repository, click Add file → Upload files
  2. Drag your index.html and photo.jpg into the upload area
  3. Scroll down and click Commit changes
  4. Your files are now stored on GitHub!

PART C — DEPLOY WITH VERCEL

  1. Go to vercel.com and click Sign Up
  2. Choose "Continue with GitHub" — this connects your accounts
  3. Click Add New Project
  4. Find your repository (e.g., my-brand) and click Import
  5. Leave all settings as default and click Deploy
  6. Wait 30 seconds — your page is now live!

YOUR LIVE URL

Vercel gives you a URL like my-brand.vercel.app. Share this on LinkedIn right away! You can add a custom domain (like yourname.com) later in Vercel's project settings.

WHY GITHUB + VERCEL?

This setup is powerful: every time you update your files on GitHub, Vercel automatically rebuilds and deploys your site. No manual uploads, no FTP — just push your changes and they're live in seconds. It's the same workflow used by professional developers.

UPDATING YOUR PAGE LATER

To make changes: go to your repository on GitHub, click on index.html, click the pencil icon to edit, make your changes, and click Commit changes. Vercel will automatically deploy the update within seconds.
